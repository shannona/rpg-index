Overview

History: 1998-2006

International RuneQuest fandom bloomed from two events: the publication of Tales of the Reaching Moon #1 (1989) and the debut of the first English-language RuneQuest convention, Convulsion of the Trillion Tentacles (1992). Many more magazines and conventions followed; soon the two trends came together, when fans began publishing fundraising books to support conventions, beginning with Questlines (1995), the fund-raiser book for RQ-Con Down Under (1996).

Enter the RuneQuest Society ("RuneQuest Gesellschaft e.V.") in Germany. They began running their own RQ-Cons in 1990, and after many years of varied venues, in 1996 they found themselves at Castle Stahleck for RuneQuest Con VII. The location stuck and RuneQuest Con IX (1998) recognized something new was going on with its subtitle: "Tentacles over Bacharach". Tentacles soon became the name for the Stahleck event, even retconning the name back to 1996.

In 1998, the German RQ Society (and the associated international Chaos Society) decided to publish their first fund-raiser book, for Tentacles over Bacharach. They put out a call for contributions and then produced a hefty 112-page book, which they called Ye Booke of Tentacles (1998). When Ye Book of Tentacles Volume 2 (1999) appeared the next year, to raise funds for Tentacles Reanimated (1999), it had the only full-color cover for the series, and it also made it clear that the fund-raiser had become a magazine of sorts. Four more volumes appeared, though they became erratic in the twenty-first century, with the two Scenario Specials both appearing in 2003, then the sixth and final issue in 2006.

The erratic publication of the later issues was in part due to the publication of Pavis & Big Rubble Companions as alternate fund-raisers, running from 2001-2004. However by 2004, a pending fan policy was hanging over the head of all of Gloranthan fandom, causing some magazines to be put on hiatus. It took until 2006 for the idea of a "Boldhome Press" to appear, which would allow Issaries to easily license official fanzines. Ye Book of Tentacles Volume 6 (2006) was both the first magazine to appear under the new brand ... and the last issue of Ye Booke. (Boldhome Press and Issaries would both fizzle out around the same time.)

Fabian Küchler was the editor for almost the entire run of Ye Booke of Tentacles, with help early on from Christian Einsporn and in the last issue from John Hughes. All six issues were produced by the RuneQuest Society, but issues #4 and #5 additionally presented the logo of "Tentacles Press". #6 was instead credited to "The Chaos Society", the RuneQuest Society's branding for their international products; it also bore Issaries' new and short-lived "Boldhome Press" logo.

Tentacles too came to an end in 2009, to be replaced by the Kraken. It has fund-raising books of its own, but they're singular chapbooks, the first of which was Sharper Adventures in HeroQuest Glorantha (2014), by Robin Laws. They continue to use the Tentacles Press brand.
