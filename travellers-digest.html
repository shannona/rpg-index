Overview

History: 1985-1990

DGP began publishing The Travellers' Digest in the early part of 1985. It was the fourth official Traveller magazine, and like JTAS and High Passage and Far Traveller before it, it began life as a small, black-and-white digest. In style, The Travellers' Digest took a leaf from High Passage's book by focusing each issue on a feature adventure and then supplementing that adventure with other content within the issue. However, the adventures were more than: they formed a campaign.

The grand campaign of Travellers' Digest was called The Grand Tour. The plan was to tour the entire Imperium, from the Spinward Marches to the Solomani Rim and back. From the start, the Tour went off well. The adventures slowly moved across the Imperium, uncovering pages of background material on each subsequent sector as it did.

Due to some combination of innovation and quality, The Travellers' Digest was a success. By the fifth issue, the covers were being printed in two colors, and by the ninth issue, the magazine graduated to a full-sized glossy magazine format. (That does mean that only eight out of twenty-one issues of The Travellers' Digest were actually a digest.)

Meanwhile, many of the ideas that were first seen in The Travellers' Digest reappeared in official GDW Traveller publications. First was Book 8: Robots (1986); afterwards, the DGP staff were a major force behind the MegaTraveller (1987) revision of the core Traveller rules. Throughout most of its life, The Travellers' Digest was integrally tied to the cutting edge of Traveller development.

In addition to The Travellers' Digest, the DGP group also published a number of high-quality Traveller and MegaTraveller supplements, often weaving ideas from them in and out of the Digest.

Near the end of 1990, the Grand Tour finally came to an end in the twenty-first, and final, issue of The Travellers' Digest. It was not the end for DGP's Traveller magazine, however. Scant months later, DGP published the first issue of its new magazine, The MegaTraveller Journal. Which would unfortunately be much more short-lived.

Obtaining the Magazines

The Travellers' Digest was well-advertised and distributed throughout its lifetime, but the digest-sized issues definitely had smaller distributions than the later full-sized ones. The earliest issues tend to go for high collector's prices while somewhere around the transition to the full-sized issues they drop to medium collector's prices.

After DGP closed up house, its entire library was bought out by Roger Sanger. He has never been able to come to agreement for any reprinting or use of the material with any Traveller publisher, and so the entire DGP library remains out of print today (and is considered "semi-canon", meaning that it can't be referenced, but for the most part shouldn't be contradicted either).

About the Contents

This updated index is a revision of a slightly less detailed one that I originally wrote in the '90s. I verified lots of game systems, locales, and other details in the original issues, but it's likely that there are still imperfections. Please feel free to email any corrections.
