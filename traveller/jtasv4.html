Overview

History: 2019-Present

Journal of the Travellers' Aid Society, or JTAS has regularly appeared from Traveller's major publishers and licensees as the official magazine of Traveller articles. GDW published JTAS v1 back in the earliest days of Traveller, and it was here that the setting of the Third Imperium took shape. JTAS v2, from Imperium Games, was a somewhat sad affair, in part because the editor and most authors didn't have a strong handle on the Traveller setting and in part because it ran only two issues. JTAS v3, from Steve Jackson Games, had a very long run, publishing on a weekly or biweekly basis from 2000-2015 and totalling some 1,500 articles total. But, because it was a website-driven collection of electronic articles, it's largely been lost to history other than the PDF book The Best of JTAS (2004).

But after the end of Steve Jackson Games' license in 2015, there was an opportunity for the publication of a new JTAS. At that point, Mongoose Publishing was Traveller's only major roleplaying licensee. And, they'd even published a periodical previously, Signs & Portents (2003-2013), a magazine supporting all of Mongoose's games, including Traveller, with the latter articles being largely collected in a pair of Traveller Compendiums (2010, 2011).

The biggest problem was that traditional periodicals were largely defunct in the 21st century. (Even Signs & Portents had run as a free PDF-only periodical during much of its run.) But Luke Crane of Kickstarter offered a solution in November 2018 when he announced the 'Zine Quest initiative, to run in February 2019. His idea was that roleplaying designers could produce old-school 'zines funded by Kickstarter patrons. Most creators produced one-off 'zines, but some instead funded multiple "issues". Most notably this included Rob Eaglestone and his Xboat (2019-2022) fanzine for Traveller5. Using the Kickstarter he funded the production of four issues and a Special Supplement, planned to be released quarterly, but actually released over the course of a few years due to the challenges of the COVID pandemic. It wasn't exactly a classic periodical, since it was prefunded and produced as a group, but it was perhaps the closest thing in the modern age.

Mongoose did not take part in the 'Zine Quest, but a month later, on March 7, 2019, they offered a very close reflection: they would produce three issues of a new, fourth volume of Journal of the Travellers' Aid, to be released as a slipcased set to patrons, and later as standalone issues. Through stretch goals, those three issues grew to six. On November 5, 2021, Mongoose repeated their Kickstarter, now funding issues #7-9 and an exlusive print copy of Bu & Embla's Guide to Starports of the Marches, but again grew that by three issues. In fact, the second Kickstarter was more successful than the first, raising ¬£118,994 from 1,146, where the first had brought in ¬£64,324 from 901.
