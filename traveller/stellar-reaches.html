Overview

History: 2005-Present

After the death of Imperium Games, Traveller entered the Long Night, a period when no one was advancing the original Traveller ruleset, though a few reprints did exist from Far Future and from QLI. Simultaneous with that, the great fanzine era of the '90s guttered out. Traveller Chronicle (1993-1997) was the first to go, a direct result of Imperium's collapse, but more notably the entire HIWG organization began to fade. The HIWG-adjacent fanzine Signal-GK (1989-1997) also ended its publication in 1997, then HIWG's three corre newsletters, AAB Proceedings (1989-1999), Starburst (1991?-1999), and The Meshan Saga (1995-1999) all shut down in 1999. For the first time since the publication of Working Passage (1984-1985) 15 years earlier, Traveller had no dedicated fanzine or magazine support.

Mind you, the internet had grown in importance as HIWG and the traditional fanzines had declined. That included email distributions such as the Traveller Mailing List (1987-Present), forums such as Citizens of the Imperium (2001-Present), and websites such Downport and Freelance Traveller. Of them, Freelance Traveller was the closest thing to a fanzine in the era, since it regularly published new articles. But it would take until middle of the '00s for a proper fanzine to arise once more.

A few different people kicked around the idea of a new Traveller fanzine on Citizens of the Imperium, among them Richard Tongue and Matt Finch, who were at various times instead be editors for the OSR fanzines OD&Dities (2000-2004, 2010) and Knockspell (2009-2011), respectively. Then Jason Kemp, Flynn on Citizens of the Imperium forums, appeared, and he shortly got Traveller's first new fanzine in several years going: Stellar Reaches (2005-2018).

Stellar Reaches was the first of a new generation of digital fanzines: never produced in print, and mostly given away for free from various websites. It was also the first fanzine to support QLI's T20 game (2002). In fact T20-fan Kemp really put T20 front and center in Stellar Reaches by setting his fanzine in the Gateway era and focusing on the Empty Quarter, which was adjacent to the Gateway domain that QLI was then featuring in Gateway to Destiny (2004). With that said, Kemp was also willing to provide support for Classic Traveller. This was all courtesy of a pair of "Fair Use" licenses available from fanzines, one from QLI, the other from Far Future Enterprises.

The Empty Quarter data was based on UWPs released by Joe Fugate of DGP to GEnie in 1989, then expanded by Alvin Plummer as part of the HIWG in 1996 and converted and further expanded by Jeff Zeitlin of Freelance Traveller in 1997. The 27 issues of Stellar Reaches would then develop this work even further, to the point that the Empty Quarter of 993 is likely the most developed locale in all of Charted Space, with literally thousands of pages of details.

There were some plans to branch Stellar Reaches out into other places, beginning with the Hinterworlds in the TNE 1248 era, but those plans would never be fuflilled. That's because Jason Kemp's time with Stellar Reaches was ultimately limited. By 2006, he'd founded Samardan Press to publish some of his work commercailly, and by 2009, after Mongoose Publishing had gained a license and publisher their own Traveller (2008), he'd begun to produce commercial Traveller products under their OGL and Traveller Trademark License, beginning with Flynn's Guide to Alien Creation (2009) and Flynn's Guide to Magic in Traveller (2009). Stellar Reaches became a lower priority, with a full two years elapsing between the publication of Stellar Reaches #7 (Spring 2007) and Stellar Reaches #9 (Summer 2009).

Fortunately, Stellar Reaches had been supported by a handful of additional writers, one of whom was no other than Alvin Plummer, who had done some of the foundational work on the Empty Quarter back in the '90s. He took over as editor with Stellar Reaches #10 (Autumn 2010). Though the 'zine would continue to bear the "Samardan Press" logo that it had adopted with #9 (replacing an older "FLTGames" logo), Plummer would be the one in control of Stellar Reaches for the rest of its life.

Alvin Plummer ran Stellar Reaches through issue #27 (Spring 2018). He was the sole contributor for almost the entirety of that run, which contained bulky issues, some of them exceeding 100 or even 200 pages, The focus continued to be the Empty Quarter in 993, but now using the Classic Traveller system, rather than the now-defunct T20 system. Some of the content was very deep dives of this setting, including a multiple part history of the area, running to about 125 pages and hundreds of pages of NPCs, particularly in the latter issues which were largely filled with them.

Plummer also offered ATUs, such as an 1145 Empty Quarter setting without a Rebellion, a religion-focused Gateway era, and his own Starbreak Sector. Stellar Reaches was largely focused on whatever interested Plummer, but most often that was Empty Quarter and its many NPCs and starships.

Plummer has long run a Stellar Reaches blog at https://stellarreaches.wordpress.com/ to support the magazine. Though the last issue of Stellar Reaches was published in 2018, the blog has continued into the present day.

Obtaining the Magazines

All 27 issues of Stellar Reaches are freely available at the Stellar Reaches website.
