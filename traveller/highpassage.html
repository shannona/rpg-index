High Passage & Far Traveller Index

Overview

History: 1981-1983

High Passage magazine was created by the High Passage Group of Jim Cunningham, Craig Johnson, and Scott Walschlager, who were high-school friends that were fans of Traveller; they turned a graphic-art class project into a professional magazine courtesy of a license from Marc Miller. The result was Traveller's second professional magazine, following GDW's own Journal of the Traveller's Aid Society (1979-1985). It was very nearly Traveller's second periodical of any type, but the British fanzine Alien Star 1981-1982), also produced by high school students, preceded the April or May debut of High Passage by a few months.

High Passage #1 (1981) lost the Group money, because they didn't know how roleplaying distribution worked. Fortunately, they were located in Illinois, which allowed them to make connections to FASA and the Keith Brothers, who were all producing Traveller material of their own. As a result, the next four issues of High Passage were proofread, laid out, and distributed by FASA and the Keiths.

By the time High Passage #5 (1982) rolled around, Johnson and Walschlager were headed off to the air force, and so the Group was becoming less able to produce a regular magazine. As a result, J. Andrew Keith and William H. Keith Jr. took over as the editors of the magazine.

Disagreements ultimately resulted in High Passage coming to an end following that issue, but FASA and the Keiths kicked off a new magazine, Far Traveller, which picked up where they'd left off, carrying over some of the columns they'd created for the previous magazine. Unfortunately, that was short-lived too, as Far Traveller trailed off after issue #2 (1983), by which time FASA had begun publishing a roleplaying game of their own, Star Trek: The Roleplaying Game (1982). Dale Kemper prepared a full manuscript for Far Traveller #3, but it was never published, demonstrating the rapidity of changes at FASA. Some of the material he prepared for the Far Frontiers Sector ended up published in Ares Magazine Special Edition #2 (1984).

Obtaining the Magazines

High Passage #1 (1981) was published in about 1,000 copies and has always been very hard to obtain. However the following four issues, all produced by FASA, were released in sufficient quantity that they remained highly available for decades afterward on the secondary markets. Even today they can be purchased at reasonable prices, especially issues #3-5. Marc Miller further supported the market in 2020 when he released a "High Passage Bundle" through Far Future Enterprises, which included copies of #2-5 taken from GDW's archives, plus a facsimile of issue #1.

Far Traveller seems to have been released in slightly lower quantities than High Passage. It was relatively easy to obtain as late as the early '90s, but today sells for low collectors' prices.

There are currently no official electronic copies of High Passage or Far Traveller.

About the Contents

This index covers both High Passage (labeled "HP") and Far Traveller (labeled "FT"), as there was a continuum between the two magazines.
